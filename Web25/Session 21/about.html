About IBSF

The International Billiards & Snooker Federation (IBSF) is the official organization that governs Snooker and English Billiards all over the world. The World Billiards & Snooker Control Council was formed in 1971, which was later renamed as the International Billiards & Snooker Federation in 1973. The IBSF has almost 100 affiliated countries that are approved by their National Olympic Committee or Sportsâ€™ Governing Authorities in their respective countries. The IBSF Board has four executive officers, President, Vice-President, Secretary General and Treasurer who are elected by the IBSF member for a tenure of four years. These four elected executives are responsible for the day-to-day functioning of the Federation and are answerable to the IBSF members. In addition to that the board also has a representative from each Region. The aims and objectives of the IBSF as a Governing Body are to co-ordinate, promote, develop and organize World and International championships for the sports of English billiards and snooker all over the world. The IBSF also determines the championship conditions, updates the rules from time to time and formulates the yearly calendar of all World and International championships. As a World Governing Body, the IBSF reserves the right to approve and sanction all kinds of international championships related to Snooker and English Billiards. The IBSF is the official World Governing Body for Snooker and English Billiards recognized by the IOC through WCBS which is a member of ARISF (Association of IOC Recognized International Sports Federations) under the patronage of IOC.
